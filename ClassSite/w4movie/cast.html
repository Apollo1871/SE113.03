ABOUT CAST REVIEW

Cast

Humphrey DeForest Bogart

Humphrey DeForest Bogart was an American actor and cultural icon known for his roles in 1940s films like Casablanca and The Maltese Falcon. His performances portrayed a hardened adventurer with a hint of idealism.

Ingrid Bergman

Ingrid Bergman was a Swedish actress with a five-decade career who is considered one of cinema's most influential figures. She won three Academy Awards, two Emmys, and a Tony Award for Best Actress. Bergman was known for her roles in American films, including Gaslight (1944), Anastasia (1956), and Murder on the Orient Express (1974), where she won Best Supporting Actress.

Paul Henreid

Paul Henreid was an Austrian-American actor, director, producer, and writer. He is best remembered for several film roles during the Second World War, including Capt. Karl Marsen in Night Train to Munich, Victor Laszlo in Casablanca and Jerry Durrance in Now, Voyager.

William Claude Rains

William Claude Rains was a British and American actor whose career spanned almost seven decades. He was the recipient of numerous accolades, including four Academy Award nominations for Best Supporting Actor, and is considered one of the screen's great character stars who played cultured villains during the Golden Age of Hollywood.

Arthur "Dooley" Wilson

Arthur "Dooley" Wilson was an American actor, singer and musician who is best remembered for his portrayal of Sam in the 1942 film Casablanca. In that romantic drama, he performs its theme song "As Time Goes By". Wilson was a drummer and singer who led his own band in the 1920s, touring nightclubs in London and Paris.
